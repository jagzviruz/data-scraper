Dyslipidaemic Agents

Dyslipidaemic agents or Antihyperlipidaemic agents or Anti-cholesterol drugs are agents that promote reduction of lipid (fat) levels in the blood. They include a class of drugs called statins and fibrates. Some agents aim to lower the levels of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol, some reduce triglyceride levels, and some help raise the high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol. They are mainly used to prevent and treat atherosclerosis (a condition in which fat gets deposited inside the blood vessels) and coronary vascular disease.
