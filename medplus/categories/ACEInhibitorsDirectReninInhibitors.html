ACE Inhibitors / Direct Renin Inhibitors

ACE inhibitors are a type of medications that act on the Renin-Angiotensin pathway, blocking the effect of renin on the blood vessels and heart, there by dilating (widening) arteries to lower the blood pressure and make it easier for the heart to pump blood. They also block some of the harmful actions of the endocrine system that may occur with heart failure. They are mainly used for treatment of high blood pressure and heart failure. Direct renin inhibitors block the enzyme renin from triggering a process that helps regulate blood pressure. As a result, blood vessels relax and widen, making it easier for blood to flow through the vessels, which lowers blood pressure. Direct renin inhibitors are used to lower blood pressure.
